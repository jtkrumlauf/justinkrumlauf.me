Hackathons

HackDartmouth V - *WINNER* - April 2019
Lil' Lexie - The Alexa Rap Battle Engine

Lil' Lexi was made by me and a team of 3 others at HackDartmouth V. Our goal behind the hack was to create a bot that is able to have a small rap battle with the user. We wanted Alexa's response to the user relate to what they said, as well as rhyme and make sense. We achieved this by using a trigram tagger from the Natural Language Toolkit to find important parts of speech, such as nouns and verbs, from the user's input. We then used the Datamuse API to allow us to find words related to the determined important words. Finally, we used a Context Free Grammar to generate two main phrases using the previously found words.

BrickHack V - February 2019
Hapi - The Twitter Sentiment Analyzer

Hapi uses various services offered by Google Cloud Platform to analyze the sentiment of certain hashtags or users on Twitter. Our goal while making Hapi was to create an easy-to-use tool that can quickly give brands or users the average sentiment of their account or brand. Hapi also provides the user with other important information such as the most negative tweet, most positive tweet, and number of tweets analyzed. This project was my first introduction to Flask and Google Cloud Platform, both of which I plan to use in the future.
